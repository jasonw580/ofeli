Detailed Description

The child class ACwithoutEdgesYUV implements a function to calculate specifically speed Fd based on the Chan-Vese model, a region-based energy functional.

The regularization of our active contour is performed by a gaussian smoothing of phi so we are interested uniquely by the external or data dependant term of this energy functional.
\(F_{d}=\lambda _{out}\left[ \alpha \left( Y_{out}-C_{outC}\right) ^{2}+ \beta \left( U_{out}-C_{outU}\right) ^{2}+ \gamma \left( V_{out}-C_{outV}\right) ^{2}\right] + \lambda _{in}\left[ \alpha \left( Y_{in}-C_{inY}\right) ^{2}+ \beta \left( U_{in}-C_{inU}\right) ^{2}+ \gamma \left( V_{in}-C_{inV}\right) ^{2}\right]\)

  • \(F_{d}\) : data dependant evolution speed calculated for each point of the active contour, only it sign is used by the algorithm.
  • \(Y\) : luminance component Y of the (Y,U,V) color space of the current pixel of the active contour.
  • \(U\) : chrominance component U of the (Y,U,V) color space of the current pixel of the active contour.
  • \(V\) : chrominance component V of the (Y,U,V) color space of the current pixel of the active contour.
  • \(C_{out}\) : mean of the intensities or grey-levels of the pixels outside the curve, i.e. pixels \(i\) with \(\phi \left( i\right) >0\).
  • \(C_{in}\) : mean of the intensities or grey-levels of the pixels inside the curve, i.e. pixels \(i\) with \(\phi \left( i\right) <0\).
  • \(C_{out}\) : mean of the intensities or grey-levels of the pixels outside the curve, i.e. pixels \(i\) with \(\phi \left( i\right) >0\).
  • \(C_{in}\) : mean of the intensities or grey-levels of the pixels inside the curve, i.e. pixels \(i\) with \(\phi \left( i\right) <0\).
  • \(C_{out}\) : mean of the intensities or grey-levels of the pixels outside the curve, i.e. pixels \(i\) with \(\phi \left( i\right) >0\).
  • \(C_{in}\) : mean of the intensities or grey-levels of the pixels inside the curve, i.e. pixels \(i\) with \(\phi \left( i\right) <0\).
  • \(\lambda _{out}\) : weight of the outside homogeneity criterion in the Chan-Vese model.
  • \(\lambda _{in}\) : weight of the inside homogeneity criterion in the Chan-Vese model.
  • \(\alpha\) : weight of the luminance component Y.
  • \(\beta\) : weight of the chrominance component U.
  • \(\gamma\) : weight of the chrominance component V.
